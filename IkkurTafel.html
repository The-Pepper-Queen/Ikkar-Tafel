If you don't know, you have 2 options:

  • If there is a “specific” (not shehakol) bracha that would technically work for all things in the mix, you make that bracha. Examples:
    • If you have a 50/50 mix of diced pineapple and orange, since the less specific bracha of Hoadama works for oranges too, you can make an hoadamah on the mix.
    • If you have a chocolate covered nut/raisin, (and you do not feel the chocolate is the ikar) technically, the brocha of Haeitz would work on the chocolate too, so you can make an haeitz on the entire thing. Technically this would work for peanuts also as Hoadamah would also technically work for chocolate.
  • If one can separate the two, one can make a bracha on each part individually. Example:
    • Crispix cereal, where one side is made from rice flour (Mezonot) and one sidefrom corn flour (shehakol) one would split it in half and make a blessing on the rice side(darker) and then the corn side (lighter).
